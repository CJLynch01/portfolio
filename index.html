About Me

Hi, I'm Christopher Lynch, a passionate software engineer with a focus on web development and database management. I'm currently pursuing a Bachelor's degree in Software Development at BYU Idaho and plan to graduate in 2025, with a GPA of 3.7. My academic journey has further strengthened my skills and commitment to excellence in software engineering.

Before transitioning to a career in software development, I served as a federal law enforcement officer, a role that taught me the importance of discipline, integrity, and attention to detail. These values have greatly influenced my approach to coding, where I strive to create efficient, secure, and reliable solutions.

With experience in technologies like HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Node.js, and Python, I've built a range of web applications that showcase my skills in both front-end and back-end development. I'm particularly interested in projects that involve data-driven decision-making and dynamic user interfaces.

My journey in software engineering started with a fascination for how technology can transform ideas into reality. Over the years, I've honed my skills through various projects, academic courses, and real-world experiences. My background in law enforcement gives me a unique perspective on the importance of security and efficiency in software development.

When I'm not coding, I enjoy reading, spending time with my wife and three children, working out and staying physically fit, going to concerts, traveling, and experiencing new things. These hobbies keep me grounded and inspired, providing a well-rounded perspective on life and work.

If you're looking for someone who is committed to excellence, eager to learn, and ready to tackle challenging problems, I'd love to connect and discuss how we can collaborate.
